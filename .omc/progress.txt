SOURCE NOTE
  2MASS-J / SDSS dropped: tile-based deep surveys hang in SkyView at >=8 deg FOV
  (must stitch thousands of small tiles). Replaced with DSS2 Blue (different band)
  and Mellinger Red (all-sky optical RGB mosaic, wide-field DSLR-class instrument
  -> closest analogue to real star-tracker imagery). Precisely: DSS2 Red and DSS2
  Blue are two photographic BANDS of the same Schmidt-plate survey (one instrument
  family); Mellinger is a separate instrument. So this is 3 image products from 2
  genuinely distinct instruments -- meeting the ">=2 distinct sources" requirement.
  All four sweep axes reuse the SAME 4 base sky fields (re-fetched per FOV, or
  re-augmented for noise/streak), so n=4 distinct fields throughout: the numbers
  are indicative trends, not precise rates.

RESULTS
  US-001 Source sweep (4 fields, FOV=10, tol 0.5 deg, seed 42):
    Fields: (7.85,-48.80) (82.54,63.07) (236.82,14.12) (220.48,-47.39)
    DSS2 Red       TETRA 4/4 (100%)  Pyramid 2/4 (50%)
    DSS2 Blue      TETRA 4/4 (100%)  Pyramid 1/4 (25%)
    Mellinger Red  TETRA 4/4 (100%)  Pyramid 4/4 (100%)
    All TETRA solves within ~0.03 deg of truth; det=20 (centroid cap) on every image.
    FINDING: TETRA is fully source-agnostic (works from unit vectors). Pyramid is
    much stronger on Mellinger (clean wide-field stars) than DSS (deep plates pick
    up galaxies/blooming that pollute Pyramid's brightest-star seed pairs).

  US-002 FOV sweep (4 fields, DSS2 Red, same DB, FOV vs accuracy):
    FOV= 8 deg   TETRA 4/4 (100%)  Pyramid 2/4 (50%)
    FOV=10 deg   TETRA 4/4 (100%)  Pyramid 2/4 (50%)
    FOV=12 deg   TETRA 3/4 ( 75%)  Pyramid 2/4 (50%)
    FOV=15 deg   TETRA 2/4 ( 50%)  Pyramid 0/4 ( 0%)
    No DB rebuild; --fov passed straight to demo_centroid_compare.exe. det=20 on all.
    FINDING: On REAL images TETRA degrades gracefully 8->15 deg, opposite to the
    100% synthetic FOV=15 in CLAUDE.md. Synthetic bypasses centroiding+camera model;
    real 15 deg has coarser angular resolution (0.017 deg/px vs 0.011 at 10 deg) and
    more faint/non-catalog blobs crowding the top-20. Real-image sweet spot = 8-10 deg.
    (Small n=4: numbers are indicative, trend is monotonic and clear.)

  US-003a Gaussian noise (4 fields, DSS2 Red, FOV=10, sigma vs accuracy):
    sigma   0%   TETRA 4/4 (100%)  Pyramid 2/4 (50%)
    sigma   2%   TETRA 4/4 (100%)  Pyramid 2/4 (50%)
    sigma   5%   TETRA 4/4 (100%)  Pyramid 2/4 (50%)
    sigma  10%   TETRA 4/4 (100%)  Pyramid 2/4 (50%)
    sigma  20%   TETRA 3/4 ( 75%)  Pyramid 2/4 (50%)
    FINDING: TETRA robust to additive Gaussian noise through 10% of dynamic range;
    first failure at 20% (1 of 4 fields lost). The DoG bandpass + area/open filter
    suppress uniform noise well. Pyramid unaffected by noise within its base rate.

  US-003b Satellite streaks (4 fields, DSS2 Red, FOV=10, REALISTIC 3-5px width):
    0 streaks   TETRA 4/4 (100%)  Pyramid 2/4 (50%)
    2 streaks   TETRA 4/4 (100%)  Pyramid 2/4 (50%)
    4 streaks   TETRA 4/4 (100%)  Pyramid 1/4 (25%)
    8 streaks   TETRA 4/4 (100%)  Pyramid 0/4 ( 0%)
    FINDING: With realistic trail widths (>=3 px) TETRA is fully robust to streaks,
    100% even at 8 simultaneous trails; TETRA solve error unchanged (<=0.024 deg).
    WHY: the centroid extractor's DoG (fine 3x3 minus coarse 11x11) is ~0 along the
    uniform middle of a thick trail (both blurs equal), so a streak only responds at
    its tips/edges; and any long streak component has area>500 px and is dropped by
    the [4,500] filter. So realistic trails rarely occupy the top-K=20 slots.
    Pyramid still degrades (seed-pair voting fragile: 0/4 at 8 trails).
    NOTE: an earlier run used 1-3px trails (half-width 0 allowed a 1px line) and
    showed TETRA failing at 4-8 streaks -- that was a test artifact: 1px ridges
    survive DoG yet fragment under morphology into spurious slot-stealing blobs.
    The verifier flagged the unrealistic width; the corrected test reverses the
    conclusion. Lesson: streak WIDTH dominates the outcome, not count.
